Edward Jovanovski (born June 26, 1976) is a professional ice hockey defender in the National Hockey League who is currently an Unrestricted Free Agent (NHL). He is of Macedonian ancestry and was born in Windsor, Ontario. He was a member of the Ontario Hockey League’s (OHL) Windsor Spitfires for two seasons, receiving First All-Star, Second All-Star, and All-Rookie Team honours. The Florida Panthers chose him first overall in the 1994 NHL Entry Draft. He was named to the All-First Team during his rookie season and helped the Panthers reach the Stanley Cup Finals, where they were defeated by the Colorado Avalanche. Jovanovski was moved to the Vancouver Canucks in a seven-player deal for Pavel Bure after three and a half seasons in Florida. He won the Babe Pratt Trophy three  years in a row as the team’s top defenseman during his time with Vancouver. He also topped the team’s defensemen in scoring for four years in a row. Jovanovski signed with the Coyotes as an unrestricted free agent in July 200During his first three years with the club, he was the team’s leading scorer among defensemen. He had three 40-point seasons and one 50-point season in the NHL as a two-way defender. Jovanovski represents Canada at the international level. He competed in the 2002 Winter Olympics in Salt Lake City and earned a gold medal. He won gold in the 1995 World Junior Championships for under-20s. He went on to win silver in both the 2005 and 2008 Men’s World Championships. He also played in the 2004 World Cup for Canada, but was only able to participate in one game due to injuries during the championship-winning campaign.
