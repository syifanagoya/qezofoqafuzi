FAQs about Reid Nibley

When was he born and when did he pass away?

He was born on January 5, 1923, and passed away on February 25, 2008.

What significant achievements did he have as a young musician?

By the age of 11, he performed with the Glendale Symphony Orchestra. At 17, he played as a soloist with the Los Angeles Philharmonic, setting the foundation for his remarkable career.

What were some highlights of his teaching career?

He taught at the University of Utah, University of Michigan, and the University of Southern California. At BYU, he was a piano professor and pianist-in-residence for 16 years. He also taught at the Interlochen National Music Camp.

What compositions did he create?

He composed numerous pieces, including the well-known Latter-day Saint hymn I Know My Father Lives.

What awards did he receive for his work?

Nibley was named Utah Performing Artist of the Year in 1982-83 and was honored with a scholarship fund at BYU.

What educational qualifications did Reid Nibley earn?
